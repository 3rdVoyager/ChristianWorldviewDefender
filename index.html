Logical Fallacies

Ad Hominem

Attacking the person’s character rather than their argument.

"You only believe in the Bible because you're a closed-minded person."

Strawman

Misrepresenting an opponent's position to make it easier to attack.

"Christians hate science because they believe the world was made in six literal days."

False Dilemma

Acting as if only two choices exist when there are actually more.

"Either God is all-powerful and can't stop evil, or He is good and just isn't powerful enough."

Hasty Generalization

Making a broad claim based on a very small or unique sample size.

"I knew a Christian who was a hypocrite, therefore all Christians are hypocrites."

Genetic Fallacy

Arguing that a claim is true or false based solely on where it came from.

"You only believe in God because you were raised in a Christian home in America."

Circular Reasoning

When the conclusion of an argument is used as one of the premises.

"The Bible is true because it says it’s the Word of God, and God wouldn't lie."

Non-Sequitur

A conclusion that does not logically follow from the previous statement.

"Religion has caused wars, therefore God does not exist."

Self-Refuting

A statement that makes itself false by its own standard.

"There is no such thing as absolute truth." (This claim asserts itself as an absolute truth).

Equivocation

Using the same word in two different ways to mislead.

"Evolution is just a theory, and gravity is a theory, so they have the same level of uncertainty."

Red Herring

Introducing an irrelevant topic to divert attention from the main issue.

"Why are we talking about the Resurrection? Look at all the bad things the Church did in the Crusades!"
